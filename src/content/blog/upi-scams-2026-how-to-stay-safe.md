## Quick answer

Never approve a collect request you did not initiate, never share OTP or UPI PIN, and verify payment intent before tapping confirm. Most fraud losses are preventable with a 10-second check.

If you remember only one line, remember this: receiving money never requires entering your UPI PIN. The PIN is only for sending money.

## Why UPI fraud is rising

UPI has made digital payments instant and easy, but scammers also move faster now. Fraud attempts today are usually social engineering first, technical attack second. They pressure you with urgency, fear, or fake authority.

### Typical emotional triggers scammers use

- "Your account will be blocked in 10 minutes."
- "Complete KYC now to avoid penalty."
- "You won a cashback reward, claim now."
- "I sent money by mistake, please return immediately."

## Most common UPI fraud patterns

1. Fake refund or KYC update links.
2. QR codes that trigger unintended payments.
3. Screen-sharing scams to capture PIN flow.
4. Urgent payment impersonation via messaging apps.

## Red-flag checklist before every payment

1. Did I initiate this payment myself?
2. Is the receiver name exactly correct inside the app?
3. Is the amount and purpose expected?
4. Is anyone forcing urgency over call or chat?
5. Am I being asked for OTP, PIN, or remote access?

If any one answer feels wrong, stop the transaction.

## Safe payment routine

1. Confirm recipient name in the app.
2. Match amount and purpose before approval.
3. Avoid unknown links and APK downloads.
4. Enable app lock, SIM lock, and transaction alerts.

## Real-world safe flow (30 seconds)

1. Open your UPI app directly, not from a message link.
2. Verify bank account and receiver handle.
3. Speak amount aloud before entering PIN.
4. Recheck last 4 digits of account/UPI ID.
5. Approve only after final confirmation screen.

## What to do in the first 15 minutes after a fraud event

- Call your bank helpline and request transaction freeze/dispute.
- Raise complaint inside the UPI app immediately.
- Report the incident on the national cybercrime portal.
- Save screenshots, caller number, and payment reference ID.
- Inform family members to ignore follow-up scam calls.

## If you are scammed

1. Contact your bank and UPI app support immediately.
2. Report on the cybercrime helpline and portal.
3. Block compromised accounts and change credentials.
4. Save screenshots and transaction references.

## Habits that reduce risk long-term

- Keep a separate low-balance account linked to UPI.
- Use daily transaction limits based on your normal use.
- Turn on instant SMS and app alerts.
- Update your payment apps and phone OS regularly.
- Never install unknown apps during "support" calls.

## FAQ

### Can someone debit my account with only my UPI ID?

Not directly. Debits still require your approval and PIN.

### Is scanning a QR always safe?

No. Scanning can open payment requests. Always verify merchant and amount.

### Should I keep a separate bank account for UPI?

It is a good risk-control practice for frequent transactions.

### Is a collect request always dangerous?

Not always, but it must match a transaction you expect. If you did not initiate it, decline it.
